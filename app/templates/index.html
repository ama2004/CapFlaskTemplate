STEM Overview



What is STEM?

Science Technology Engineering Mathematics
Science is using observations and experiments to gain knowledge about how the universe works, such as chemistry. Science can help us understand how the world works and improve our quality of life. Technology is an application of scientific knowledge that can help solve problems, such as computers. Engineering is using scientific processes to design and build technology and infrastructure, such as robots. Mathematics is using numbers and formulas to understand patterns, to quantify relationships, and predict the future, such as arithmetic.


STEM Gap

The STEM gap is the underrepresentation of women and minority groups in STEM.

Sexism, racism, stereotypes in STEM

Women and people of color experience discrimination, especially exployment discrimination + work discrimination when pursuing STEM. Among those in STEM, 72% of blacks say a major reason why blacks and Hispanics are underrepresented in these jobs is because they face discrimination in recruiting. Among those experiences, some 45% of black STEM workers say that they have had someone treat them as not competent because of their race. Many women and minorities face negative stereotypes and expectations creating more pressure for them. Research indicates that both women and people of color are more likely to report discrimination, microaggressions, and harassment in STEM.

Income Difference in STEM Jobs

Generally, women are paid less than men in STEM jobs, but there are some exceptions. Wages aren't only related to workplace issues. An example of this is when women with children are shown to have higher wages than women who do not because women with high earning potential are able to balance family and work life.

Education in STEM

Sometimes it is not the lack of STEM education that is causing people to not consider STEM. Some people are just not that interested in STEM. Some are but were encouraged by others, such as academic advisors, to pursue non-STEM related majors. It could also be that there are some underfunded schools that lacks STEM education. Microaggressions towards women and people of color in college and the workplace, which can be race, gender, and possibly even sexual orientation. An example would be being asked to do other tasks that are not related to their job responsibilities.

Why does it matter?

Closing the STEM gap matters because these factors can create less diversity in STEM due to the pressure created and more people would drop out from STEM because they feel out place because of their race and gender. This makes the experience not welcoming to people who are underrepresented in STEM. This means that girls, women, and people of color are less likely to stay in STEM and pursue other non-STEM careers.



Benefits to STEM

Having a strong foundation in STEM offers a lot of benefits. It helps develop your critical thinking and communication skills. It also improves the individual's confidence and competence when they know how to solve a problem. STEM classes can help prepare people real application of those skills for not only STEM careers, but careers in other sectors. They allow students to experiment and experience failures, which can then help build resilience. The skills learned in STEM can be used in other subjects and in an interdisciplinary way.
